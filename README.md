Insights:
Roughly 1 out of every 6 employees leaves the company, indicating a moderate attrition problem.
Although R&D experiences the most attrition numerically, it also has the largest workforce. Management should investigate R&D and Sales teams to identify retention issues.
Younger professionals appear more likely to switch jobs for better salary growth, career opportunities, or work-life balance.
Employees with lower salaries are more likely to leave.This suggests compensation may be a major driver of attrition.
Technical and sales-related positions face the highest turnover. These roles may require improved incentives, career progression plans, or workload management.
Married employees form the largest portion of the workforce. Further analysis can determine whether marital status impacts attrition rates.
